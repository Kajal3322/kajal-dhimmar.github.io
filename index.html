About

The analyst
behind the data

I came to supply chain planning through an unusual door — a Master's in Operations Research, then another in Data Analytics. Most planners think in spreadsheets. I think in systems. That changes how I see a problem.

I'm most useful in the gap between raw data and a decision. Where someone has a forecast that isn't working and doesn't know why. Where a planning process exists but nobody trusts the output. That's where I dig in — not just to fix the number, but to understand the system producing it.

Outside of work I'm drawn to patterns in complexity — which is probably why demand planning felt like a natural fit. Every SKU is a small puzzle. Every variance is a signal worth reading.

Design for Health
Inherited a forecasting function with no handover — rebuilt it from scratch, learning Anaplan independently, gaining backend access, and restoring a process that had effectively stalled for months.
Traced a months-long gap in forecast uploads to files that had never been properly updated or validated. Investigated, cleaned, and restructured data across 7,000+ SKUs to produce the first leadership-approved forecast load in over six months.
Reduced the active SKU base from 7,000+ to roughly 4,300 by auditing and discontinuing inactive, duplicate, and obsolete products — improving forecast reliability, inventory allocation, and reporting clarity across all six channels.
Working closely with my manager and Anaplan experts, uncovered that the WMAPE metric was being systematically inflated — not by bad forecasting, but by duplicate SKUs and a misconfigured backend formula. Corrected both together, cutting reported forecast error nearly in half and establishing a reliable accuracy baseline for the first time.
Built the company's first standardised monthly forecast report covering 5,000+ SKUs across all six channels — shipment history, forward forecast, open orders, backorders, and performance metrics in a single reusable template. Received direct recognition from senior leadership for the improvement in forecasting quality.
Maintained a discontinuation tracker monitoring inventory levels against real demand to time SKU exits without creating overstock — and kept the item master accurate so every downstream team was working from the same source of truth.
Prepared the data foundation for a Power BI reporting migration and contributed to building the company's first SKU-level budget forecast for the following fiscal year — restructuring and automating datasets that had previously required fully manual handling each cycle.
